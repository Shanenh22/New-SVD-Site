A dental implant is a titanium post placed in the jawbone that permanently replaces a missing tooth, it looks, feels, and functions like a natural tooth and lasts decades with proper care. Why are dental implants better than dentures or bridges?

A dental implant is the closest thing modern dentistry has to a natural tooth. It's a titanium post placed in your jawbone that fuses over time and becomes the anchor for a crown, bridge, or denture. It looks, feels, and functions exactly like your own tooth, and with proper care, it lasts for decades.

At Spring Valley Dental, Dr. Aletaha places and restores implants in-house. You won't be sent to a specialist and handed off between offices. One team, one experience, from start to finish.

Am I a candidate? Most adults with good general health qualify for implants. The key factors are sufficient jawbone density and healthy gums. If bone has been lost after a tooth extraction, bone grafting can often restore the volume needed for a successful implant. We assess this at your consultation and explain every option, there is no obligation.

Why implants outperform other options

Permanent, not removable like dentures
Preserves jawbone (dentures and bridges let bone deteriorate)
No grinding down of healthy neighboring teeth (unlike bridges)
Easy to clean, brush and floss like a natural tooth
Success rate above 95% at 10 years with proper care
Can replace one tooth or a full arch

What does the dental implant process look like, step by step?

1

Consultation & evaluation

We examine your jaw, take 3D imaging, and give you an honest assessment of candidacy and expected costs. No pressure.

2

Implant placement

The titanium post is placed in the jawbone under local anesthesia. Most patients are back to normal activities the next day.

3

Healing & osseointegration

Over three to nine months, the implant fuses with your jawbone. This is what makes it permanent and stable.

4

Crown placement

Once healed, we attach a custom porcelain crown matched to your surrounding teeth. You leave with a complete smile.

Dental implants vs. bridges vs. dentures, which is right for you?

Every tooth-replacement option has tradeoffs. Here's an honest side-by-side comparison so you can make the right decision for your situation.

Factor Dental Implant Dental Bridge Dentures
Feels like a natural tooth Yes Mostly No
Preserves jawbone Yes No No
Requires altering adjacent teeth No Yes No
Expected lifespan 15–25+ years 10–15 years 5–10 years
Removable No (fixed) No (fixed) Yes
Surgery required Yes (minor) No No
Relative upfront cost Higher Moderate Lower

The best choice depends on your bone density, budget, health history, and personal preferences. We'll discuss every option honestly at your consultation, including which one we'd recommend for your specific situation.

Frequently asked questions

A single implant including the post, abutment, and crown typically ranges from $3,500–$6,000 in the Dallas area depending on the complexity of the case. If bone grafting is needed, that adds cost. We review your specific pricing clearly at your consultation before any commitment. CareCredit and Cherry financing make monthly payments manageable for most patients.

Some PPO plans cover a portion of implant costs, particularly the crown. Many plans that were 'implant exclusion' plans 10 years ago now have partial coverage. We verify your benefits before your appointment and tell you exactly what to expect.

The placement procedure is performed under local anesthesia and is typically less uncomfortable than patients expect. Most people compare the recovery to a tooth extraction, mild soreness for a few days, managed with over-the-counter pain relief. Sedation is available for anxious patients.

From placement to the final crown, most implant cases take three to nine months. The majority of this is healing and osseointegration, the titanium post fusing with the jawbone. Once healed, the crown placement is a straightforward appointment.

Bone grafting can often create the bone volume needed for a successful implant. We evaluate this at your consultation. Not every patient who has been told 'no' at another practice is truly not a candidate, it's worth getting a second opinion with us.

Implant vs. bridge, which is right for you?

A bridge uses adjacent teeth for support and typically lasts 10–15 years. An implant stands alone, preserves bone, and does not involve neighboring teeth. Most patients who qualify for implants prefer them long-term, but we walk you through both options at your consultation so you can make the right decision for your situation.
