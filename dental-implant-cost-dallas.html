How Much Do Dental Implants Cost in Dallas, TX?

A transparent 2026 guide to implant pricing in the Dallas area, honest ranges, what drives the cost, and how to make it affordable.

Medically reviewed by Dr. Shokooh Aletaha, DDS · Updated June 2026

Dental implants are one of the best long-term investments you can make in your health, but the price is almost always the first question patients ask. This guide breaks down what dental implants really cost in the Dallas area in 2026, what drives the price up or down, and how to make treatment affordable. As always, the only way to get an exact number for your situation is a consultation, but you deserve honest ranges before you ever walk in the door.

Average dental implant cost in Dallas

In the Dallas–Fort Worth area, a single dental implant, including the titanium post, the abutment, and the porcelain crown that sits on top, typically ranges from $3,500 to $6,000. That range covers the complete tooth replacement, not just one component. Larger or full-mouth restorations cost more because they involve multiple implants and more complex planning. Here is a rough picture of what different treatments tend to run:

  • Single implant (post + abutment + crown): $3,500–$6,000
  • Implant-supported bridge (replacing several teeth): $6,000–$10,000
  • Full-arch implant solutions: $20,000 and up, depending on the technique
  • Bone graft (if needed before placement): $400–$1,200 per site

If a quote you receive elsewhere seems far below these numbers, ask exactly what is included. A low “implant” price sometimes covers only the post, with the abutment and crown billed separately later.

What affects the price of a dental implant

No two mouths are the same, and several factors move the final cost up or down:

  • Number of teeth being replaced. One implant is straightforward; multiple implants or a full arch require more planning, materials, and chair time.
  • Whether you need a bone graft. If the jawbone has lost density after a tooth was missing for a while, a graft may be needed to support the implant securely.
  • The crown material. High-quality, natural-looking porcelain costs more than basic materials but lasts and looks better.
  • Extractions or other prep work. If a damaged tooth still needs to be removed, that is a separate step.
  • Imaging and planning technology. 3D cone-beam imaging improves accuracy and safety and is part of doing the job right.

Does insurance cover dental implants?

A growing number of PPO dental plans now cover a portion of implant treatment, often a percentage of the crown or a set annual maximum, though many still classify implants as elective. The honest answer is that it depends entirely on your specific plan. At Spring Valley Dental we verify your benefits before treatment begins and tell you exactly what your plan will and will not cover, so there are no surprise bills.

Financing options that make implants affordable

For the portion insurance does not cover, we offer CareCredit and Cherry Financing. Both let you spread the cost over low monthly payments, and many patients qualify even with less-than-perfect credit. Patients without insurance can also join our in-house membership plan for 20% off treatment.

Spring Valley Dental pricing context

We believe in transparent, no-surprise pricing. At your consultation we take 3D imaging, give you an honest assessment of whether you are a good candidate, and hand you an exact, itemized cost, with your insurance coverage already factored in. There is never pressure to proceed, and we will always tell you if a less expensive option would serve you just as well. You can read more about the procedure itself on our dental implants page.

The bottom line: plan on roughly $3,500–$6,000 for a single complete implant in Dallas, confirm your exact number at a consultation, and use insurance plus financing to make it manageable.
