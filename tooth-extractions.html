When is a tooth extraction actually necessary?

Nobody should lose a tooth that could have been kept. Before any extraction, we look hard at the alternatives, root canal therapy, a crown, gum treatment, and show you exactly what we see on the imaging. If the tooth can be saved at a cost and prognosis that make sense for you, that's our recommendation. Extraction is the right call only when keeping the tooth no longer serves you: severe decay below the gumline, an unrestorable fracture, advanced gum disease, or a failed prior treatment.

When extraction is the answer, the experience is designed around comfort. The area is fully numbed before we begin, you'll feel pressure rather than pain, and you leave with written aftercare instructions and a direct line to us if anything feels off during healing.

Honest scope: we perform simple and routine extractions in-office. Wisdom teeth and complex surgical extractions are referred to trusted oral surgery specialists, we coordinate the referral and handle your follow-up and replacement planning here.

What's included

A genuine save-vs-remove evaluation with imaging you can see

Conscious sedation available for anxious patients (Dr. Houshmand, certified)
Written aftercare instructions, in English or Spanish
Replacement planning: implant, bridge, or partial, explained with honest costs

Why replacement planning matters

A removed tooth leaves more than a gap. Neighboring teeth drift, the opposing tooth over-erupts, and bite forces redistribute, slowly, then expensively. Except for teeth that genuinely don't need replacing, we'll walk you through the options before you're in the chair: a dental implant for the most natural long-term result, a bridge when neighboring teeth need crowns anyway, or a partial denture as a budget-conscious path. No pressure, just the full picture, with financing if you need it.
